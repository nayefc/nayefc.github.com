What Bitcoin Fans Are Missing

Bitcoin fans, rightly so, quickly jump to accuse the world's financial system as clunky and obsolete. There is no doubt. NPR ran an a great episode under their Planet Money blog titled The Invisible Plumbing Of Our Economy, which outlines some very surprising facts about the ACH system in the U.S.

I'm going to avoid diving into they typical Bitcoin arguments (real vs. fake value, privacy etcâ€¦), and point out one extremely important argument often overlooked by Bitcoin fans.

Financial systems are un-innovative, slow and clunky not because the people who run them are stupid, but apart from benefiting from the current system and fees, it is due to risk.

Risk shaped most of the modern financial sector as we know it today. With Bitcoin, how will the government and organisations assess one's credit worthiness and their ability to pay their debt? The whole system will collapse. Risk is very important, and sadly often ignored by those who have not worked extensively in the field. It is the main reason why the financial systems are old and slow, why their procedures are bureaucratic, and why most startups fail in this field. So far, no one has been able to revolutionise the way risk analysis works.

What we need is a network like Dwolla or Ripple and not a new currency. Perhaps a virtualised currency run by a central bank or a central authority. The world is practical and pragmatic. Bitcoin is Utopia-like. Looks great on paper, but not in real life.

Who is willing to take on the risk? No one.
